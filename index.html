Our Mission

Empowering community members where they live, learn, and lead.

Three Wise Women empowers community members by providing microgrants, fostering civic engagement, and hosting fundraisers that build local capacity.

We were founded on a simple belief: the strongest social safety net is a neighbor who shows up. Every program we run — from springtime Easter basket distributions to year-round volunteer organizing — is designed to remove friction between people who want to help and the families who need help most.

We don't position ourselves above the community. We work alongside it.

What We Do

Three programs. One promise: show up for our neighbors.

M

Community Microgrants

Small, fast-turnaround grants for individuals and grassroots groups solving immediate, tangible needs — from a single-parent rent shortfall to a youth-led mural project. Decisions in weeks, not months.

C

Civic Engagement

Town-hall facilitation, voter registration drives, and community listening sessions that bring residents into local decisions — especially residents who don't usually sit at the table.

E

Easter Basket Drive & Public Events

Every spring we distribute Easter baskets to children across the region, paired with year-round fundraisers and public events that engage more than 1,000 volunteers across our service area.

Impact

Local trust, measured in turnout.

Volunteer hours and basket counts aren't vanity metrics — they're how we know the community is showing up for itself.

In our first year of programming, more than 1,000 community volunteers have engaged with Three Wise Women — distributing Easter baskets, staffing public events, organizing micro-fundraisers, and helping evaluate microgrant requests from neighbors.

Every dollar we raise stays close to home. Every program we run is owned by the people it serves. We measure success by whether the people in our towns feel less alone — not by our office square footage.

Leadership

Meet our founder.

SN

Sarah Nelson

Founder & Chief Executive Officer

Sarah Nelson founded Three Wise Women in 2025 to formalize years of grassroots community organizing across North Stonington and southeastern Connecticut. A long-time civic volunteer with deep ties to local government and the neighborhood-aid networks of the region, Sarah leads the foundation's microgrant decisions, civic engagement programming, and volunteer coordination.

She holds the principal officer role on the organization's IRS Form 990 filings and serves as the foundation's primary point of contact for partners, donors, and the press.
